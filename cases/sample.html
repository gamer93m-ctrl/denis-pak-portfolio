Overview

Sample Merch is a production platform where individuals, companies, and government clients can order custom clothing and branded merchandise. The platform handles everything from item configuration to production tracking and delivery.

The existing checkout flow had high abandonment rates due to an unclear ordering process and no visibility into production stages after purchase. My goal was to simplify the flow and add transparent progress tracking.

Platform overview / before state

Research & Methodology

I reviewed session recordings and exit surveys to identify where users were dropping off. I also conducted 5 interviews with B2B clients (corporate buyers) and 4 with individual customers to understand their different mental models around the ordering process.

Key findings:

  • Corporate buyers needed a formal request/approval flow — not a standard cart
  • Individual customers abandoned when they couldn't estimate delivery time upfront
  • Both groups wanted clear production stage visibility after placing an order
  • The 24-hour manager response promise was a strong trust signal but wasn't visible enough

Design Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1 — Uncluttered, logical checkout

The previous checkout had 7 steps with confusing branching logic for different order types. I reorganised it into a 3-step linear flow with smart defaults for returning customers, and separated the B2B corporate request path from the standard individual order flow.

Checkout flow — before and after

Challenge 2 — Merchandise request system for B2B

Corporate buyers need to submit requests, get internal approval, then confirm the order. I designed a dedicated B2B request flow with a shareable quote view and a clear approval status screen — making the process match how procurement actually works in large organisations.

B2B request flow
Quote / approval view

Challenge 3 — Production stage tracking

After placing an order, customers had no visibility until delivery. I designed a transparent production timeline with 5 clear stages and real-time status updates — reducing support tickets and building trust in the production process.

Production tracking — order status screens

Challenge 4 — 24-hour response promise

The platform's key differentiator was guaranteed manager response within 24 hours. I made this promise prominent in the UI — visible on the product configuration screen, the cart, and the order confirmation — turning a policy into a conversion driver.
